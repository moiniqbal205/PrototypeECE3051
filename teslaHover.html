Tesla Induction Motor

For an induction motor, its speed depends on the frequency of the AC power supply; thus, varying the frequency of the power supply enables us to alter the drive wheel speed - reliable and easy speed control


IC can only produce usable torque and power output only within limited speed range - a significant disadvantage


On the other hand, an induction motor will work efficiently in any speed range

Inside the Motor

The induction motor has two main parts -- stator (receives 3-phase AC power input) and the rotor (collection of conducting bars short-circuited by end rings)


The 3-phase alternating current in the coils produces a rotating magnetic field, which induces current on the rotor bars to make it turn


An induction motor has neither brushes nor a permanent magnet, thus, making it robust and powerful



The induction motor :

Weighs significantly less than the IC engine

Has a much higher output power-to-weight ratio (8.5 kW/kg)

Has no harmful emissions

The IC engine :


Requires additional parts for rotational motion + more

Inefficient power-to-weight ratio (~ 0.8 kW/kg)

Causes harmful emissions
